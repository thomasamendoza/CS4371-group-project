A Look at Some Popular Breeds

Australian Shepherd

Known for their intelligence, high energy, and striking merle coats, Australian Shepherds are excellent herding dogs and active companions. They thrive with owners who can provide plenty of physical exercise and mental stimulation.

Pembroke Welsh Corgi

Instantly recognizable by their short legs, long body, and foxy face, Corgis are affectionate, smart, and surprisingly athletic. They have a big-dog bark in a small package and make loyal family pets.

Maltese

These small, gentle dogs are covered in a long, silky white coat. Maltese are playful, charming, and thrive on human companionship. Despite their aristocratic bearing, they enjoy playtime and make great lap dogs.

American Shorthair... Cat?

Wait a minute... this isn't a dog! Looks like someone snuck a cat onto the list! The American Shorthair is a popular feline breed known for its easygoing temperament, robust health, and classic tabby patterns. While not a canine, they certainly make wonderful companions too! (Someone might need to double-check their breed list next time!)
