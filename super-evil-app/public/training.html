Training a dog starts with building a strong bond rooted in trust, patience, and consistency. Begin with the basics—commands like sit, stay, come, and leave it. Use positive reinforcement such as treats, praise, or play to reward good behavior. Dogs thrive on encouragement, so celebrate their wins (even the small ones). Keep training sessions short and engaging—about 5 to 10 minutes at a time—to avoid overwhelming them.

Consistency is key. Use the same words, gestures, and tone each time you give a command, and make sure everyone in the household is on the same page. Avoid punishment-based techniques; instead, redirect unwanted behavior and reward the positive. Every dog learns at their own pace, so be patient. With time, love, and dedication, your pup will not only be well-behaved but also more confident and happy.
